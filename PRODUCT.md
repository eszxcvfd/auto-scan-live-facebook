# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single operator uses LiveScout on a desktop computer when they need to find public Facebook livestreams about a topic. The operator searches on demand and needs fast, trustworthy results rather than analytics or a multi-user workflow.

## Product Purpose

LiveScout helps an operator discover publicly searchable Facebook broadcasts that are live at search time. It requires a keyword, verifies candidate broadcasts before displaying them, excludes recordings and replays, and sends the operator to Facebook to watch the selected broadcast.

Discovery coverage is best-effort. The product does not claim to inventory every livestream on Facebook.

## Brand Personality

Focused, trustworthy, precise.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards filled with unrelated widgets or metrics.
- Noisy neon interfaces that compete with the search task.
- Result archives that blur the difference between live broadcasts and recordings.
- In-app video playback that suggests the product owns or guarantees the Facebook broadcast.

## Design Principles

1. **Show verified state, not assumptions.** A result is presented as live only after its current state has been checked.
2. **Search is the primary task.** Keep the interface centered on entering a query, scanning results, and opening a broadcast.
3. **Make freshness visible.** Show when a result was verified and avoid implying that live status persists forever.
4. **Respect the source boundary.** Facebook remains the place where broadcasts are watched; LiveScout is the discovery layer.
5. **Prefer useful coverage over false completeness.** Clearly communicate best-effort discovery instead of presenting an incomplete inventory as exhaustive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use semantic HTML, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, readable contrast, descriptive link names, and reduced-motion support. Loading, empty, error, and success states must be understandable without relying on color alone.
